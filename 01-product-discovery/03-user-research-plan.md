# 03 User Research Plan

## Objective

The goal of this research is to understand how enterprise investigators currently conduct investigations, identify the challenges they face when working with fragmented evidence, and explore opportunities where AI can improve investigative workflows without replacing human decision-making.

Rather than validating a predefined solution, this research aims to discover real user problems that can inform product strategy and feature prioritization.

---

## Research Questions

This study focuses on answering the following questions:

### Workflow

- How do investigators conduct an investigation from start to finish?
- What tools and systems are involved?
- Which activities consume the most time?

### Pain Points

- What are the biggest frustrations during investigations?
- Where do investigators experience the highest cognitive load?
- Which tasks remain highly manual?

### Information Management

- How do investigators organize evidence?
- How are timelines reconstructed?
- How are relationships between entities identified?

### AI Adoption

- Which investigation tasks are users comfortable delegating to AI?
- Which tasks require human judgment?
- What concerns do users have regarding AI-generated outputs?

---

## Research Assumptions

Before conducting research, the following assumptions have been identified.

These assumptions will be validated or rejected through user research.

| ID | Assumption |
|----|------------|
| A1 | Investigators spend significant time manually organizing evidence. |
| A2 | Timeline reconstruction is one of the most time-consuming activities. |
| A3 | Existing AI tools improve search but not investigative reasoning. |
| A4 | Users require complete evidence traceability before trusting AI outputs. |
| A5 | Human investigators want AI assistance rather than autonomous decision-making. |

---

## Research Participants

The research focuses on three primary stakeholder groups.

| User Group | Purpose |
|------------|---------|
| Corporate Investigators | Understand day-to-day investigation workflows and pain points. |
| Compliance Managers | Understand governance, explainability and audit requirements. |
| Investigation Analysts | Understand repetitive operational tasks and evidence organization. |

---

## Research Methodology

Because this project is currently at the Product Discovery stage, qualitative research methods are prioritized.

Research methods include:

- Semi-structured interviews
- Workflow analysis
- Competitive product analysis
- Secondary research from industry reports, product documentation and public case studies

Primary interviews help uncover user behaviors and unmet needs, while secondary research provides additional industry context and validates recurring patterns.

---

## Expected Outputs

The research findings will be synthesized into the following deliverables:

- User Personas
- Current User Journey
- Jobs To Be Done (JTBD)
- Problem Statement
- Opportunity Hypotheses

These artifacts will guide product strategy and define the scope of the initial MVP.

---

## Success Criteria

The research will be considered successful if it enables the team to:

- Clearly understand the end-to-end investigation workflow
- Identify the highest-impact pain points
- Validate or reject the initial product assumptions
- Prioritize opportunities for AI-assisted investigation
- Define the target users for the MVP
